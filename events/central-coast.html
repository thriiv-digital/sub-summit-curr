About Suburban Business Summit

Suburban Business Summit is a one-day event for local business owners, professionals, and community leaders who believe strong local businesses build strong communities.

We bring "big conference" ideas, speakers, and connections into suburban and regional areas, close to where you live and work. Our first Summit showed there's real demand for practical, no-hype learning and genuine local connections.

In 2026, we're growing into a series of local events across Australia, each with its own community flavour but the same goal: helping local businesses grow, collaborate, and lead. You'll find people from trades, charities, finance, professional services, health, retail, hospitality, education, and more.

At each Summit you can expect:

  • Simple, practical ideas you can use straight away
  • Real conversations and relationships that last beyond the day
  • A focus on how suburban businesses power Australia's economy and communities

Suburban Business Summit is where local ambition meets practical support. Backing business. Driving success.

Location

Gosford Regional Function Centre

Level 1, Gosford Library

123a Donnison St, Gosford NSW 2250 (click for Google Maps)

Getting There

By train: Gosford Station is an easy 5–7 minute walk from the library. Exit towards the CBD and follow the signs to Donnison Street.

Drop-off: There is a short-term drop-off area at the front of the library on Donnison Street, ideal for passengers or accessibility drop-offs.

Access Inside the Venue

The event is in the function/auditorium space on Level 1. Access is available via both stairs and a lift from the ground floor.

All public areas have step-free routes, with an accessible entrance and accessible toilets on the ground level.

Parking

The closest large car park is Gosford City Car Park, 1 Baker Street, a short walk to the library.

There is limited street parking on Donnison Street and Mann Street, so please allow extra time to find a space.

For all-day/free parking, you can use commuter car parks at Adcock Park or Racecourse Road, West Gosford, then walk or take public transport into the CBD.

We recommend arriving a little earlier to allow time for parking and the short walk to the venue.
